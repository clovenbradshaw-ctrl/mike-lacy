What I’m Starting

A Ground Truth Cohort. Let’s call it a working group — not a class, not a bootcamp. I don’t have anything to teach, just some questions I’ve sharpened by asking them wrong in the past. It’ll be for people who want to build real projects with public data and develop a shared framework for testing the validity of each other’s work.

Wednesdays, 7–9 PM, starting in a few weeks. Bring a project. An eviction analysis, a budget deep-dive, a public records scraper, whatever you’re curious about and willing to show.

We’ll ask each other hard questions:

  • Where did your data come from, and how did it enter the record?

  • What’s your frame, and what would someone in a different position see?

  • Does your conclusion survive contact with a different perspectives, or does it depend on assumptions you haven’t examined?

You don’t need to know fancy data science tools R or Python. You need to be curious, willing to show your work, and open to being wrong. Nashville people especially welcome, but the framework should work anywhere. The legitimacy crisis is not local.

Cost: Free | Sign up: email me

I’ll be vetting people who I think make sense to join at this point, it’ll be small, and sorry if you ask to sign up but aren’t in the first one.

I’ll be subjecting my own work to the same scrutiny. That’s the whole point. The ground truth is not a thing you find. It’s a thing we triangulate — together, transparently, with the humility to know no perspective can see the whole truth, but that with enough of us, we can get closer and closer.
