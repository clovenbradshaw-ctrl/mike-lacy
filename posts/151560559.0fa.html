I don’t have a lot to say about what happened last Tuesday. That might be surprising to some, I’m usually nothing if not verbose. In 2016, and then in 2020 with COVID, I fell into an “activation” savior mode that wound up emotionally exhausting me — and likely didn’t do much good for the world.

This time I have more of sense of what I value and want to see in the world, I’m trying to just find the stamina to do that a bit more. I want to tell you about one group I work with in particular that could use your support, and is precisely the type of organization that will do the most good for the most vulnerable these next few years.

Workers Dignity (or Dignidad Obrera in Spanish) is a wage theft recovery non-profit in Nashville that works primarily with Latinx non-native English speakers.

My first gig after leaving the Nashville Mayor's Office was working part-time for Workers Dignity, and since then I've been volunteering helping them modernize their data processes — and I have nothing but glowing things to say about their ability to serve this extremely at-risk class of people.
