A multilingual winter resources guide has been launched by the Substack account Rich Text and Ground Truth Nashville. It is available at groundtruthnashville.org, bit.ly/help4nash and a QR is available below.

This app serves as a multilingual gateway to emergency and community resources in Nashville, supporting English, Spanish, Arabic, Kurdish, and Vietnamese with full right-to-left layout for Arabic and Kurdish readers. Rather than hosting content directly, the app links out to existing resources from organizations like Nashville.gov, TEMA, FEMA, Second Harvest, and immigrant community services—launching them through Google Translate so pages remain accessible in the user’s preferred language. The app also pulls real-time weather data and translates NWS weather alerts, ensuring critical safety information reaches non-English speakers.

The tool is already featured on the homepage of Where To Turn in Nashville.

The tool also includes an integration with the open source NES Power Outage Tracker developed by GitHub user NeckBeardPrince. Our implementation adds translation to the 4 most common non-english languages in Nashville. Cudos to the developer for building the insightful tool.

Anonymized analytics are collected via webhook to track general usage patterns—including language and text size preferences, resource link clicks, weather fetches, and emergency button usage—to help improve the app’s accessibility and usefulness over time. No location data or personal information is, nor can be, collected.
