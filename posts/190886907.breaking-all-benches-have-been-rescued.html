NASHVILLE, Tenn. — At approximately 13:21 hours Central Daylight Time, an elite, four person squadron of community organizers concluded a daring extraction operation, successfully liberating all detained seating platforms from the notorious detention facility colloquially known as “bench jail” — a stronghold deep within the perimeter of the Nashville Department of Transportation compound, situated in the 700 block of S 5th Street in of East Nashville.

No bench was left behind.

The surgical extraction came roughly 24 hours after it was revealed that the city had been detaining the beloved bus stop accessories without provocation — and with conflicting narratives as to the reason for their confinement.

Photos of the rescue mission were released on official channels via the Instagram @abench4everyone, accompanied by a message from the organization behind the bench-building endeavor.

The official statement was accompanied by a strongly worded warning from a person confirmed by contemporaneous photographs to be among the rescuers.

No benches were harmed in the tactical extraction. The reputations of many city leaders were not so lucky.
