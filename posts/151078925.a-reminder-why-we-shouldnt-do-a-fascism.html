The impulse to hand authority over to a single, fully empowered individual or small insular group to make prompt decisions in times of crisis. The word “strong man” is used often for this figure, a real mean daddy that is going to take care of everything.

For when fascists come, when those narcissistic tyrannical daddy figures arrive looking bigger than life, they instinctively know how to manipulate that insecurity. The term “Dictator” was a specific job given to a leader by the Ancient Roman Senate. It gave this person full powers to make any decisions, with two caveats: (1) they could make no permanent changes to Roman law; (2) the had to step down after 6 months.

Julius Caesar was the third Roman dictator who paid with his life for not respecting his term limit.
