Nashville has a rare opportunity to leverage a $15M investment into the downtown corridor for a wide range of uses. How would you like to see that money spent?

The District Management Corporation (DMC) which oversees the central business improvement district (which covers most of what you’d consider “downtown”) got a $15M grant from the State of Tennessee.

Take the Survey!

As I’ve been discussing at length, there seems to be a big push by the Mayor’s Office & MNPD to use much of this money for surveillance and policing technology. They did this without notifying anyone that this money was available, and without even notifying their new “Community Safety Task Force” members that this was their plan.

In fact, it seems like they waited to even create this task force until after they’ve pushed for millions of dollars in new surveillance and policing gear.

This is all incredibly concerning given the recently revealed coordination between people like TN House Speaker Cameron Sexton and the White House’s Stephen Miller to shape our state immigration laws and public safety. Miller is not exactly who most Nashvillians want to be helping shape our community and public safety policy.

If our local leadership isn’t intentionally carrying water for expanding ICE’s surveillance state in Nashville, then they doing a helluva great job of doing it by accident.

But here’s the thing: the money doesn’t have to be used for any of that. In fact, the DMC was awarded the money for a very broad set of uses. The folks at the Nashville Community Safety Network have put together a survey to get feedback about what Nashvillians really want downtown.

Take the Survey!

The survey is anonymous, but it does ask for your Council District so that Metro Council Members, the people who ultimately have control on the finances of the District Management Corporation, know precisely what their voters want these $15M to be used for.

But then it will be up to Metro Council to decide how they feel about that when they review the CBID’s budget for FY27.

And ultimately, if the money does flow to policing and surveillance technology, it will be up to Nashville residents and tourists to decide whether they're comfortable with the downtown businesses they patronize funding systems that are impossible to keep out of ICE's hands.

A quick reminder, we’ve had a lot of good results communicating with the public about companies that are working with ICE and why people shouldn’t do business with them. It will be all the easier when those businesses are based here in Nashville.
