Today, communities across the country are gathering to observe National Homeless Persons Memorial Day, remembering those who died while experiencing homelessness.

At 1 PM CT, the National Healthcare for the Homeless Council will stream their national ceremony, featuring original music performed by Nashville’s own Daniel Holmes and Kennetha Patterson, artists and activists who bring the weight of lived experience to this day of remembrance.

In Nashville, that weight is measured in names: 174 people experiencing homelessness have died in our city this year, according to India Pungarcher of Open Table Nashville. Each of their names will be read aloud at Saturday’s memorial at the riverfront—at 1st and Broadway, where the city has designated a space to honor the unhoused.

Bobby Watts, CEO of the National Healthcare for the Homeless Council, will speak at both events. His son, Stephen Watts—whose extraordinary testimony before Metro Council this week urged accountability over surveillance expansion—will join him at Saturday’s gathering.
