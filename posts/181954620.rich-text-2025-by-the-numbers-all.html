2025 By The Numbers

In 2025, {Rich Text} published 186,261 words across 94 posts—almost exactly the length of The Fellowship of the Ring (187,790 words), and in the same neighborhood as Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire. That’s roughly 310 single pages, or about 510 words per day, every day, for a year. Stacked against traditional journalism, it’s over 230 standard newspaper articles, produced without an editor, a newsroom, or a salary. The average post ran just over 2,000 words—longer than most op-eds, shorter than most people’s patience for local government accountability reporting.

All of it written in coffee shops, Switchyards, at my kitchen table, and far too often at 2 AM on my couch when a public records request finally came through.

Below is a note from me about this past year. Scroll past that and you can find all articles I published this year organized by topic. Many are series that are best read in the sequences provided.

What 2025 Has Meant for Me

2025 has been… a year. It started off inauspiciously.

On New Years Day 2025, I was in Mexico City with my partner and friends and I got food poisoning. I was sick enough that I had to be hospitalized and given an IV. A doctor later had to visit our AirBNB and administer another IV.

A week later there was an electrical fire in an office shed in the backyard of the home we rent. I had been using it as my home office and storage for my decades worth of videography equipment, books I've collected, hardware tools, comic books — all the physical stuff that made me me. I lost virtually all my personal possessions, besides my clothes and computer, in the fire.

And then there was everything going on in our country and city. It’s been… a year.

I’ve certainly been in some sort of fight or flight mode. My anxiety found an outlet through my fingers onto a keyboard. I disappeared into this work no one assigned to me. I thank my partner for being patient and supportive in this year of deep fixation. I would not be who I am without you. I love you.

In 2025, I made some incredible friends. I’ve been part of a movement. Several movements. I’ve been entrenched in the perennial push-and-pull of collectivist power vs elite capture of our society in a way I’ve aspired to but could never have been prepared for. I have felt fully swept up in the moment.

I’ve been interviewed for television, news articles and podcasts. I won an award. And I got a piece of journalism I wrote actually edited, fact checked, and published by a media outlet I’ve long respected.

I made plenty of mistakes. An incalculable number of tpyos. Given public comment at innumerable council meetings. Published financial records of city departments, helped make the case for internal audits. Got my work called “fake news” and “misinformation” by city leaders but helped the truth prevail nonetheless. I connected with the least and most powerful in our city (and often made the latter very upset with me).

The adage of journalism, comfort the afflicted and afflict the comfortable — I’ve come to know that vibe very well.

I’ve fought alongside people from all walks of life for change and reform, and more than once we got some good results. The folks reading this, we’ve done so much together this year. This will be a year I will never forget. In some ways I am very ready for it to go away. But it’s a year I’ll never fully let go.

There was piece of advice given to me early this year from someone who was not pleased with what I was writing. He told me something to the effect of: “people who are becoming civically engaged should be careful in how they interact with bureaucrats. Many young people who want change can be off putting when their thinking is too critical and they’re very strong minded.”

It was an honor to be offered advice from someone from such a high position of authority in our city. And also to be called a young person still at 35. But I haven’t taken his advice.

I’ve chosen not to lessen my critical thinking and to be more weak minded. I’ve leaned in to my neuroatypicality and my ADHD and my special interests.

I have learned lessons about being more patient. About holding my tongue and staying professional, even if it’s abundantly clear what I really think. Not because it’s always wrong to be brash, but because it can sometimes be more effective to not say the quiet part out loud. But there are also times where that is what is required.

In 2025, I found a role for myself in Nashville. I think it’s something we all crave, to feel like we have a purpose. I was most uncomfortable this year when I actually “broke news.” I felt most in the pocket when I said what many people knew but were afraid to say.

Every time I posted this year, I never did it with the expectation that I had to do it again.

I let myself be fully guided by my impulses. I had no publishing schedule, and never an outline of what I wanted to talk about. Any structure to my articles or series I made was developed on the fly. At times I worked with others to time things to other events. Sometimes I collaborated with other writers. But I always gave myself the option to walk away immediately from this Substack if it didn’t feel right.

And there were times where I thought “I’m done.” Sometimes it was out of anxiety over the impact of what I was writing. It is stressful to be worried if you're wrong about something important no one else is talking about. It's even more stressful to worry that you are, in fact, very right about something important no one else is talking about. Other times I thought of quitting it was out of boredom with being so damn serious. But I kept coming back.

I don’t know who I’ll be in 2026. Maybe I’ll be the same but more, or less, or maybe something entirely different and I’ll lose the support of the subscribers that like what they saw. We’ll see.

I love that people have chosen to support this work with donations. And that support is just cresting into an amount that actually might impact my tax returns and that is amazing. But part of what has made these 186k words possible is not caring about any of that — so if in 2026 I’m not who you want me to be because of who I was in 2025, I’m sorry. But know that central to what made me who you wanted this year was that I was not trying to be that at all.

But in 2025, I’ve been honored to be your Metro Watchdog.

Woof woof.

-Mike
