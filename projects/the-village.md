# The Village

A community collaboration platform designed inside Nashville's **Mayor's Office of Community Safety** (2022–2023). Built to coordinate work across 40+ agencies, manage a $5M multi-source budget across 50+ contracts, and track implementation of a [$2M federal DOJ grant](https://www.nashville.gov/departments/mayor/news/mayors-office-community-safety-receives-nearly-2-million-grant) for community violence intervention. The underlying relational database integrated city departments, service providers, and neighborhood organizations into a single operational picture — the same picture Metro used when it [routed community safety dollars to neighborhoods](https://www.nashville.gov/departments/mayor/news/more-community-safety-dollars-headed-neighborhoods) and when [grant programs opened](https://www.newschannel5.com/news/community-safety-grants-available-to-help-curb-violence-in-nashville) to the organizations already doing the work.

The office was led by [Ron Johnson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ron-johnson-8372a991/), who described the [community safety and engagement initiative](https://www.nashvillepost.com/business/education/metro-s-ron-johnson-on-community-safety-engagement-initiative/article_22106f94-40d6-11ed-b4f5-9b1eb3d91ad3.html) as a bet on coordination: letting the people closest to the violence do the work, with Metro as a backbone instead of a bottleneck. [Dawn Stone](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dawn-stone/) ran operations. My seat was the one holding the coordination layer together — the contracts, the database, the dashboards that let a program officer see at a glance which of the [21 nonprofits funded through Opportunity Grants](https://www.nashville.gov/departments/mayor/news/metro-nashville-council-approves-opportunity-grants-21-nonprofits) were on track and which needed a call.

The Village outlived city hall. It now runs as an independent, community-led initiative under the **Community Foundation of Middle Tennessee**, which [expanded its support and named Erika Burnett program manager](https://www.cfmt.org/story/community-foundation-expands-support-to-the-village-advocacy-leader-erika-burnett-named-program-manager/) in 2023. Burnett was later [promoted to Vice President of Community Development](https://www.cfmt.org/stories/erika-r-burnett-named-vice-president-of-community-development/) and now [sits at CFMT in that role](https://www.cfmt.org/staff/erika-burnett/); her own account of how she got there is [in Nashville Voyager](https://nashvillevoyager.com/interview/rising-stars-meet-erika-burnett-of-nashville/).

What The Village does today is visible in its own materials: a plain-language [overview of the initiative](https://thecontributor.org/learn-more-about-the-village/), a [Q&A with Burnett](https://thecontributor.org/qa-with-erika-burnett/) about the shift from staff-led to community-led, a longer [program description at CFMT](https://www.cfmt.org/communityleadership/community-initiatives/thevillage/learn-more-about-the-village/), and the [2024 Annual Report (PDF)](https://s3.amazonaws.com/CFMT_web_pdfs/2024/TheVillage-Annual+Report-Print.pdf). The platform I built inside the mayor's office was a scaffold. The Village is what the community built on top of it.
